## 1. Handling negatives, samples & damaged film

These are suggested policies and texts you can adjust to your own preferences.

### 1.1. When a customer sends film

Ask customers to include in the package (or in the order notes):

- Full name
- Email address
- Order reference (if you use one)
- Film type(s) and number of rolls
- Whether the film is:
  - **Normal, unexposed rolls**
  - **Test / sample negatives**
  - **Already-developed negatives for scanning only**
  - **Damaged, expired or ‚Äúmystery‚Äù rolls**

You can add a short line in your order popup description for them to clarify this:

> *‚ÄúIf you are sending already-developed negatives, test strips or damaged film, please mention it in the description box.‚Äù*

### 1.2. Policy for damaged / risky film

You can use wording like this on your website or FAQ:

> **Damaged, expired or risky film**  
> I will always treat your negatives with care, but heavily damaged, badly stored or very old film is more likely to have issues (strong grain, colour shifts, fog, scratches, missing frames).  
>  
> I will do my best to recover as much as possible, but I cannot guarantee results and I am not responsible for pre-existing damage.

Optionally, you can decide on a simple rule for payment:

- Either **full price** regardless of outcome, or  
- **Reduced price** / ‚Äúno charge if totally blank‚Äù

Write down what you want here:

- My decision: `______________________________________________`

### 1.3. Handling negative samples

If someone asks for **test scans** or sends a **sample strip**:

> **Sample or test scans**  
> If you are unsure about my style or about the condition of your negatives, you can send a small strip or one test roll first. I will scan it and send you samples before we process the rest.

You can:

- Offer **a lower sample price**  
- Or include sample scans as part of a first order

### 1.4. Returning negatives

Decide if you will:

- Always return negatives
- Return only on request
- Charge extra for return shipping

Example text for the site:

> Yes, I can return your negatives on request. Return shipping has an extra cost depending on your location. Please mention in your order if you want your negatives back.

### 2.2. ‚ÄúHow it works‚Äù ‚Äì step-by-step

This is **very important**. Keep it simple and clear:

1. **Order online**  
   Customer fills the form on your website and chooses:
   - Colour or black & white
   - Development only / scan only / both
   - Number of rolls

2. **Ship or drop off film**  
   Customer:
   - Packs the rolls in a small padded envelope
   - Adds their name & email inside
   - Ships to your address (or drops off locally if you allow it)

3. **Development & scanning**  
   You:
   - Develop the film (C-41 or B&W)
   - Scan at your standard resolution
   - Apply your usual colour/contrast look

4. **Delivery**  
   Customer:
   - Receives a **download link** to their scans via email (WeTransfer, Drive, Dropbox, etc.)
   - Optionally gets their negatives back if requested
